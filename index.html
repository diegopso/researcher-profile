About Me

June, 2018

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science in the Institute of Computing at University of Campinas. I obtained my master's degree in 2018 in this same institute. In my master I produced a framework for mobility data analysis in urban centers, namely SMAFramework. During this period I was visiting researcher for six months in University of Ottawa in a program funded by the Government of Canada, and during two months in the University of Coimbra, funded by the Santader International Bank. In these opportunities I worked in developing new tools to the SMAFramework and improving old ones. I graduated in Information Systems in the Lutheran University of Brazil in 2013. During my academic career I have published 7 papers qualified with Qualis CAPES B or superior, including paper in conferences as: IEEE ICC '18, ACM MSWiM '17, SBC SBRC '17 and '18, IADIS International Conference '13, SBC SBSC '14 and '12; and also a paper in the Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science in 2013. The paper paper sent to SBSC '14 was granted the award of second best full paper published.

My research interests involve a range of topics, like...

  • Urban Mobility
  • Smart Cities
  • Data Analisys
  • Trust Analisys in Social Networks
  • Expert Recommendation and Expertise Analisys
